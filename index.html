Client scenarios to work through
Try each scenario yourself first, then click it to see how a strong consultant would handle it.
🏢 An Advanced client wants SSO but their IT team isn’t available for 3 weeks. The timeline is at risk. What do you do?+
SSO is a client-side dependency, so flag it as a risk on the internal sync and to the client straight away. Do not let it block the rest of the build: SSO is one of the last config steps, so sequence everything else first (org structure, permissions, social accounts, approvals) and slot SSO in when IT is free. Send the SSO setup guide now so IT can prepare, and reset the timeline in writing rather than letting it slip silently. The graduation date only moves if SSO is genuinely on the critical path.
🔐 A client asks you to set up permissions for 200 users one by one. How do you handle this efficiently?+
Do not do it manually. Use the bulk CSV upload through Enterprise Onboarding with the create action, and suppress welcome emails during setup so users are not emailed before go-live. Set up the teams and permission structure first so the uploaded users land in the right place. This is exactly what the requirements doc is for: confirm the user list, teams and roles there before you touch the platform.
⚠️ A client’s social accounts keep disconnecting every few days. They’re frustrated. Explain why and resolve it.+
This is almost always an expired auth token, not a Hootsuite fault, so reassure the client it is expected. The fix is for the account owner to reconnect the profile in account settings. If it keeps happening, check whether the network revoked access (a password or permission change on the network side) or whether the connecting user lost access. Frame it as routine maintenance and show the admin how to reconnect, so they can handle it themselves.
📋 Two weeks in and the client still hasn’t filled out the requirements doc. Training is next week. How do you move forward?+
Do not start config without it, and do not let it drift silently. Escalate gently: a direct message to the client owner explaining that training and config depend on the doc, with a specific date you need it by. Offer to fill it in together on a short call, which often unblocks a client who finds the doc daunting. If it still does not come, the responsible move is to move the training date rather than train on a half-built org.
🎓 The client says they’re ready to graduate but can’t add a user or build an approval workflow. What do you do?+
Do not graduate them. Graduation means the client can run their org independently, and they cannot yet. Name the gap kindly: do a quick hands-on check in the session, see what they struggle with, and cover those specific tasks again. Point them to the on-demand Onboarding Hub to reinforce, and only graduate once they can add a user and build a workflow themselves. Graduating early is the most common mistake and it creates support problems later.
❓ During training the client asks you to create a social post for them. Should you? How do you handle it?+
Gently redirect rather than doing it for them. Admin training is about enabling the admin to manage the platform, not about you operating it, so have them create the post while you guide. Tie it to their goal: ask what they are trying to publish and why, then walk them through the steps as they do it. Doing it for them feels helpful but leaves them unable to do it alone, which undermines graduation.

What Advanced Inbox is

Inbox 2.0 is where teams reply to comments and private messages received across social networks, from one place. All Hootsuite customers get standard Inbox.

Advanced Inbox adds efficiency features for high-volume customer care teams: automations, routing and prioritisation rules, SLA tracking, business hours, CSAT surveys, an agent workspace, and (as an add-on) a Generative AI Chatbot that can answer messages automatically.

It is delivered second, after HSE, because it builds on the org, team and permission structure you set up in Hootsuite.

Key concepts to know
Standard vs Advanced
Standard = reply to comments and DMs. Advanced = automations, routing, SLAs, business hours, CSAT, agent workspace.
Builds on HSE
Inbox uses the same org, teams and permissions. Get HSE structure right first or Inbox config gets messy.
Care team mindset
Inbox clients think in response times and queues, not posts. Frame everything around SLA and agent efficiency.
Chatbot is an add-on
The Generative AI Chatbot answers messages automatically. Only configure it if it’s in the contract.

Client scenarios to work through
Try each scenario yourself first, then click it to see how a strong consultant would handle it.
⏱️ The client wants a 1-hour SLA but only staffs the team 9-5. How do you set this up correctly with business hours?+
Set business hours first, because SLAs calculate against business hours, not the wall clock. Configure the team 9 to 5 (plus any holidays) as the business hours, then set the 1-hour SLA so it only counts time inside those hours. That way a message at 4:30pm is due by 9:30am the next day, not breached overnight when no one is staffed. Walk the client through this so their targets are realistic and the reporting is not full of false breaches.
📥 Messages are landing in the wrong team’s queue. How do you diagnose and fix the routing?+
Work the routing rules in order. Check which rule catches the message first, since rules apply in sequence and an early broad rule can grab messages meant for a later one. Confirm the conditions (network, keyword, language, assigned team) match what the client intended, and that the target team has the right members and permissions. Reorder or tighten the rule that is mis-catching, then test with a live message before handing back.
🤖 The client bought the AI Chatbot but has no FAQ content. How do you guide them to launch it well?+
The chatbot is only as good as the content behind it, so do not launch it empty. Pause the rollout and help the client assemble their top question-and-answer content first, usually their existing FAQs, macros or saved replies. Start in a limited scope (a few well-covered topics) rather than everything, and set a clear human-handoff path for anything it cannot answer. Go wide only once the content covers the real volume of incoming questions.
📊 The client asks how to prove the care team is hitting targets. What reporting do you point them to?+
Point them to Inbox reporting on SLA performance and response times, broken down by team and agent. Tie it back to the business hours and SLA rules you configured, since those define what on target means. CSAT surveys add the quality side alongside the speed side. Show the admin how to pull these themselves so they can report up to their own leadership.

What Hootsuite Listening is

Hootsuite Listening lets clients search, track and monitor real-time conversations across social. It’s similar to Talkwalker but less advanced — the key difference is that Listening is fully integrated into Hootsuite, which Talkwalker standalone currently is not.

Clients use it for brand monitoring, competitor tracking, campaign measurement and spotting trends. The core skill you’re enabling them on is building good Boolean search queries that return relevant mentions without noise.

Key concepts to know
Integrated, not standalone
Listening lives inside Hootsuite. Talkwalker is the more advanced standalone product — don’t confuse the two.
Queries are everything
A good Boolean query is the difference between signal and noise. This is the core skill to teach.
Quick search vs saved topics
Quick search = ad-hoc. Saved topic insights = ongoing monitoring with alerts and reporting.
AI query builder
Clients can generate queries with AI, but you should teach them to audit and refine what it produces.

Client scenarios to work through
Try each scenario yourself first, then click it to see how a strong consultant would handle it.
🔍 The client’s brand query is returning thousands of irrelevant mentions. How do you tighten it?+
Tighten the Boolean. Add NOT clauses for the obvious irrelevant meanings (other brands, unrelated uses of the term), and use quoted phrases or proximity instead of loose keywords. Require brand context by pairing the brand term with product or industry terms so a generic word does not match everything. Refine iteratively, checking the returned mentions after each change, and teach the client to do this so they can adapt it later.
🎯 The client wants to track three competitors and a campaign hashtag but only has 5 queries (Advanced). How do you prioritise?+
Prioritise against what the client will actually act on. You do not need one query per competitor: a single well-built query can cover the brand plus close competitors using OR groups, which frees queries for the campaign hashtag and anything time-sensitive. Ask which insights drive a decision and build those first. If they genuinely need more separation than 5 queries allow, that is a package conversation, not something to fudge.
🤖 The client built a query with the AI builder and it’s missing obvious mentions. How do you audit and fix it?+
AI-generated queries look right but routinely miss edge cases, so always audit before saving. Check for missing spelling variants, abbreviations, languages and handles, and confirm the operators do what you expect. Test it against a few mentions you know should appear and see if they are caught. Treat the AI output as a first draft to refine by hand, and show the client how to spot and fix the gaps themselves.

What Advanced Analytics is

All Hootsuite customers get standard Analytics. Advanced Analytics is the purchasable upgrade that adds custom metrics, deeper reporting, ROI analysis, content auto-tagging and web attribution (Google / Adobe Analytics).

The typical AA implementation runs in four phases: Plan (handoff, kickoff), Setup (connect accounts, configure), Train (show the client how it works), Launch (graduation). Your work focuses on connecting accounts and analytics, then configuring the reporting the client needs.

Key concepts to know
Standard vs Advanced
Standard Analytics = built-in reports. Advanced = custom metrics, ROI points, autotagging, web attribution. Know the line.
Web attribution needs a unique post ID
GA/Adobe attribution only works if every published link carries a unique identifier. No ID = no post-level web data.
Autotagger isn’t retroactive
Tags only apply to new content. Backfilling old data needs a support ticket. Set this expectation early.
Scoping is optional
Autotagging and ROI changes are an optional scoping conversation after training — only if the client wants customisation.
Default ROI profile
Every social account gets a default ROI metrics profile. Never edit the default — always create a new profile.

Client scenarios to work through
Try each scenario yourself first, then click it to see how a strong consultant would handle it.
🔗 The client connected Adobe Analytics, it worked, then disconnected the next day. What’s likely wrong?+
This is the classic Adobe permissions issue: the connection can look successful and then drop within a day when the underlying access is not right. Check that the credentials have the correct product and report-suite admin access on the Adobe side, not just login access, then redo the JWT connection. It is worth scheduling a short call to do the Adobe connection together, since it is more involved than the Google flow.
📈 The client wants to compare paid vs organic ROI but is on Plus (2 reports). How do you scope this realistically?+
Scope to what 2 reports allow rather than overpromising. You can often answer the question within two well-designed reports rather than one per channel, so define the specific comparison they need and build to that. Be clear about what is in and out of scope for the package. If the full cross-channel ROI picture genuinely needs more, that is an honest upgrade conversation, not something to stretch the package to cover.
🏷️ The client wants campaign-level reporting on 50+ campaigns. Is autotagging the right answer? How do you scope it?+
Autotagging can be the right tool, but scope the effort honestly. It is JSON-configured, applies only to new content (not retroactive, so historical data needs a separate backfill request), and 50+ campaigns is real setup work, so it is an optional scoping conversation after training, not a quick toggle. Have the client complete the autotagging worksheet so the rules are precise. Set the expectation that tags start working going forward, and never edit the default ROI profile, always create a new one.
📊 Web attribution shows zero conversions for every post. How do you diagnose it?+
Diagnose in order. First confirm every published link carries the unique post identifier, since without it there is no post-level web data. Then check the links point to the domain configured in the web analytics connection, and that the goals or events are named exactly as they are in GA or Adobe. Finally, allow up to 24 hours for data to sync before concluding it is broken. Most zero-conversion cases are a missing identifier or a goal-name mismatch.

What Amplify is

Amplify is Hootsuite’s advocacy product. It lets employees share pre-approved content to their personal social profiles, extending reach through employee networks. There’s also a social-selling use case for sales teams.

Amplify projects are different from other products: they’re preparation-heavy and often phased. Success depends on content pipeline, communication planning and executive sponsorship as much as configuration. Most projects run 4-6 weeks (longer for 500+ advocates).

Key concepts to know
Preparation is everything
Amplify lives or dies on content pipeline and comms planning. Configuration is the easy part.
Dark-launch users
Add advocates via CSV without triggering welcome emails. The exec sponsor sends the launch email on go-live day.
Roles: Admin / Publisher / Contributor / Subscriber
Content Admins manage everything. Publishers post to assigned topics. Contributors need approval. Subscribers read-only.
Open vs closed topics
Open topics are discoverable by all (need 4+ pieces of content). Closed topics segment content to specific groups.
Seat tiers change the model
25-200 seats: self-guided (CAM-led). 200-999: dedicated IC. 1000+: dedicated IC + recommended strategy hours.
Strategy session matters
For larger deals, a strategy session on goals, stakeholders, comms and content runs before or early in the project.

Client scenarios to work through
Try each scenario yourself first, then click it to see how a strong consultant would handle it.
🚀 A client with 800 advocates wants to launch all at once. Why might you recommend a phased approach, and how do you structure it?+
Recommend a phased launch. Amplify lives or dies on content and engagement, so going wide on day one with thin content or no champions usually stalls. Start with an engaged pilot group (a team or department with a champion), build the content rhythm and prove value, then expand in waves. Dark-launch each wave via CSV without welcome emails, and have the executive sponsor send the launch message to each group as it goes live.
📝 The client has Amplify configured but no content ready. How do you guide them to build a pipeline before launch?+
Do not launch into an empty platform. Help the client build a content pipeline first: identify who will source and approve content, set up the three content routes (compose, send-to-Amplify from streams, and the RSS Syndicator), and get a backlog ready so advocates have something to share from day one. Open topics need at least a few pieces of content just to appear. Tie this to the strategy session, since content supply is what sustains the programme.
📧 Advocates aren’t engaging after launch. What adoption tactics do you recommend to the champion?+
Engagement is a programme problem, not a platform one, so work with the champion on the human side. Make sure there is a steady supply of relevant, easy-to-share content and that the executive sponsor is visibly participating. Use the leaderboard, suggested content and recognition to build momentum, and remind advocates of the personal benefit to their own reach and profile. Look at the analytics to see who is and is not active, and target the quiet groups directly.
🔗 The client wants UTM tracking on Amplify shares but links keep dropping images. How do you set it up correctly?+
Do not use per-post link customisation in the Amplify Composer, which is what drops the images. Set the link settings and UTM parameters as the org default instead, so tracking is applied automatically without breaking the share preview. Confirm the default is configured at the Amplify org level. Set the expectation that UTM tracking works through the org default, not by editing individual links.
